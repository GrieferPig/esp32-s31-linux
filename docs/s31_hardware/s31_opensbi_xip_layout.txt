ESP32-S31 OpenSBI Flash XIP layout

Goal:
- Keep OpenSBI text/rodata in flash and execute through the flash MMU window.
- Keep OpenSBI writable data, BSS, scratch, stack, and heap in HP SRAM.
- Keep the OpenSBI partition size fixed at 512KB.

Address contract:
- Bootloader maps the opensbi partition for instruction access at 0x40030000.
- OpenSBI must be linked with FW_TEXT_START=0x40030000.
- OpenSBI writable sections are linked with FW_RW_START=0x2F040000.
- The bootloader verifies that the mmap pointer equals 0x40030000 before mret.

Partition image format:
- Offset 0: raw OpenSBI fw_jump.bin.
- Next byte after fw_jump.bin: OpenSBI FDT blob.
- Padding: zero bytes up to partition_size - 4.
- Last 4 bytes of the 512KB partition: little-endian uint32 file offset of the FDT blob.

Bootloader handoff:
- The bootloader reads the last 4 bytes of the opensbi partition.
- It computes fdt_addr = mapped_opensbi_base + fdt_offset.
- Before mret, the bootloader disables HP_APM, HP_MEM_APM, and CPU_APM
  function filters through their FUNC_CTRL registers.
- Before mret, the bootloader clears mstatus.MPRV and clears pmpcfg0..3 plus
  pmpaddr0..15. Any locked PMP entries will remain visible in the logged cfg
  values.
- Before mret, the bootloader writes mie=0 and leaves mstatus.MIE/MPIE clear.
  OpenSBI enters M-mode with global interrupts disabled; it must enable and
  route interrupts itself after replacing the bootloader/IDF state.
- Before mret, the bootloader runs quick probes:
  - HP SRAM read/write/execute.
  - Flash XIP read/execute. Direct mapped flash write is skipped because XIP
    flash is expected read-only.
  - PSRAM read/write/execute using the uncached write alias and cached execute
    alias near the top of mapped PSRAM.
  - OpenSBI early access path:
    - .data copy source LMA in the mapped flash XIP image.
    - .data VMA at FW_RW_START in HP SRAM.
    - .bss and initial stack area in HP SRAM.
    - One AMO operation against FW_RW_START.
- Before mret, the bootloader installs a temporary mtvec trap logger. If
  OpenSBI traps before replacing mtvec, the logger prints mcause, mepc, mtval,
  mstatus, mtvec, and mscratch, then restarts.
- OpenSBI's early _start_hang path also writes a trap mailbox at 0x2F07E000:
  - word 0: magic 0x53335450
  - word 1: mcause
  - word 2: mepc
  - word 3: mtval
  - word 4: mstatus
  - word 5: mtvec
  - word 6: mscratch
  - word 7: mhartid
  The bootloader checks and clears this mailbox at the start of app_main, so a
  reset after OpenSBI's temporary mtvec=_start_hang path can still reveal the
  original OpenSBI fault PC.
- It jumps to mapped_opensbi_base in M-mode with:
  - a0 = 0
  - a1 = fdt_addr

Build-time checks:
- The generated fw_payload.bin must be exactly 512KB.
- OpenSBI + FDT must fit in the first 512KB - 4 bytes.
- Link-time assertions require FW_TEXT_START=0x40030000 and FW_RW_START in HP SRAM.

OpenSBI XIP relocation rule:
- fw_base.S copies .data from flash LMA to FW_RW_START before the boot lottery.
- The R_RISCV_RELATIVE relocation loop then patches pointers inside .data, for
  example platform.ops at platform + 0x60.
- Do not copy .data a second time after relocation. Doing so restores the raw
  flash image, zeros relocated pointers, and can fault on a null load in
  sbi_platform_ops().

Early fault note:
- OpenSBI's first instructions copy .data from flash LMA to FW_RW_START.
- A store/AMO access fault before the OpenSBI banner usually points at handoff
  protection state, not the FDT offset. Check APM FUNC_CTRL, PMP, MPRV, and
  the pre-handoff probe log.
- If the OpenSBI early access probe passes but the temporary mtvec logger
  reports a fault, decode the logged mepc against fw_jump.elf. If the CPU still
  lockups without the temporary trap log, check the next boot for the
  "previous OpenSBI early trap" mailbox print. Decode that mepc against
  fw_jump.elf.

      Based on the "Address Mapping Structure" graph in the Memory Organization
section, the memory address space is unified for both the High-Performance (HP)
CPUs (Core 0 and Core 1) and the Low-Power (LP) CPU, but with specific access
restrictions.

Here is the relationship between the address ranges and their mapped targets:

1. Shared Address Space (Accessible by both HP CPUs and LP CPU)

The following memory and peripheral regions can be accessed by any of the CPUs:

- CPU Sub-system: Mapped to 0x0000_0000 – 0x1FFF_FFFF.
- HP & LP Peripherals: Mapped across multiple fragmented address ranges:
  - 0x2000_0000 – 0x200F_FFFF
  - 0x2020_0000 – 0x202F_FFFF
  - 0x2040_0000 – 0x204F_FFFF
  - 0x2060_0000 – 0x206F_FFFF
  - 0x2070_0000 – 0x2071_FFFF
  - 0x2080_0000 – 0x2081_FFFF
- CPU Peripherals: Mapped to 0x2C00_0000 – 0x2C01_FFFF.
- Debug Peripherals: Mapped to 0x2D00_0000 – 0x2D00_7FFF.
- LP Memory (32KB): Mapped to 0x2E00_0000 – 0x2E00_7FFF.
- HP Memory (512KB): Mapped to 0x2F00_0000 – 0x2F07_FFFF.

2. HP CPU Exclusive Space (Inaccessible by the LP CPU)

The following targets are restricted solely to the HP CPUs and cannot be
accessed by the LP CPU:

- Cache Data Memory: Mapped to 0x2C04_0000 – 0x2C07_FFFF.
- ROM (320KB): Mapped to 0x2F80_0000 – 0x2F84_FFFF and accessed through the
    ROM Cache.
- External FLASH (256MB MAX, 16MB onboard):
  - Cached Access: Mapped to 0x4000_0000 – 0x4FFF_FFFF and routed through
        the 32KB Cache and FLASH MMU.
  - Direct Access: Mapped to 0xA000_0000 – 0xAFFF_FFFF and routed directly
        to the FLASH MMU (bypassing the cache).
- External PSRAM (64MB MAX, 16MB onboard):
  - Cached Access: Mapped to 0x5000_0000 – 0x53FF_FFFF and routed through
        the 32KB Cache and PSRAM MMU. (don't use for opensbi/linux bring up, use uncached)
  - Direct Access: Mapped to 0xC000_0000 – 0xC3FF_FFFF and routed directly
        to the PSRAM MMU (bypassing the cache).

3. Unavailable Address Space

The following address ranges are strictly reserved and are not available for use
by any of the CPUs:

- 0x2000_0000 – 0x202F_FFFF
- 0x2040_0000 – 0x204F_FFFF
- 0x2060_0000 – 0x206F_FFFF
- 0x2072_0000 – 0x207F_FFFF
- 0x2082_0000 – 0x2BFF_FFFF
- 0x2C02_0000 – 0x2C03_FFFF
- 0x2C08_0000 – 0x2CFF_FFFF
- 0x2D00_8000 – 0x2DFF_FFFF
- 0x2E00_8000 – 0x2EFF_FFFF
- 0x2F08_0000 – 0x2F7F_FFFF
- 0x2F85_0000 – 0x2FFF_FFFF
- 0x3000_0000 – 0x3FFF_FFFF
- 0x5400_0000 – 0x9FFF_FFFF
- 0xB000_0000 – 0xBFFF_FFFF
- 0xC400_0000 – 0xFFFF_FFFF
